How this tool works

What it does

It shows two things: what your PPO write-offs cost you per year, and, if you are analyzing one plan, how many of that plan's patients you would need to keep at full fees to match today's revenue if you dropped it.

What to gather before you start

  • Monthly insurance write-offs: run your PMS adjustment report and total the insurance adjustments for a typical month
  • Monthly production (optional): your PMS production report for the same month
  • One plan's 12-month revenue (for the exit math): a per-plan production report covering BOTH the insurance payments and the patient payments for that plan's patients
  • Average discount off your full fees: compare that plan's contracted fee schedule against your full fees, or ask your billing coordinator; many PPO contracts run 30-40% below

The math, in plain English

Write-offs are multiplied by 12 for the yearly total. For the exit math: the plan's revenue divided by (100% minus the discount) gives the full-fee value of the same care; keeping that percentage of the patients' visits at full fees is the break-even line, and anything above it is growth.

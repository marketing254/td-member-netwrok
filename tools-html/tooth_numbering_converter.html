How this tool works

What it does

It translates any tooth between the three numbering systems. Click a tooth on the arches or type a code from any system, and all three appear together with the tooth's name.

The three systems, one line each

  • Universal (US): numbers 1-32, starting at the upper right third molar; what most US insurance forms use
  • FDI (ISO): two digits: quadrant then tooth (11-48); common internationally and in research
  • Palmer: quadrant letters plus 1-8 counted from the midline (UR1, LL6); common in orthodontics and the UK

How to use it

Nothing to calculate and nothing to gather: the mapping between the three systems is fixed. Print the full chart below and pin it where insurance paperwork happens.

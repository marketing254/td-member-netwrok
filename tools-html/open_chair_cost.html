How this tool works

What it does

It totals what empty chair time actually costs per year, and what winning back even one hour a week is worth.

What to gather before you start

  • Average production per chair hour: weekly production divided by scheduled chair hours
  • Open or no-show hours per week: audit one typical week's schedule and count the holes, including reschedules that left a gap
  • Working weeks per year: 52 minus your holidays and vacation weeks

The math, in plain English

Weekly cost = production per hour x open hours. The yearly number multiplies that by your working weeks. The one-hour line shows production per hour x working weeks: what a single reclaimed hour is worth.

How this tool works

What it does

It shows what a fee increase is worth in year one after the patients you might lose, what staying flat quietly costs you as your expenses inflate, and the five-year gap between raising yearly and never raising.

What to gather before you start

  • Annual production: your year-end production report
  • Planned fee increase: the percentage you are considering; Dr Moffet raised with the cost of living every year
  • Patients you expect to lose: Dr Moffet's experience was about 1% lost per 10% raise; adjust if your market differs
  • Your yearly cost inflation: compare this year's total overhead to last year's on your P&L

The math, in plain English

New production = current production, raised by the fee increase, reduced by the patient loss. The hidden pay cut = production times your cost inflation (costs rise while flat fees do not). The five-year line compounds the yearly raise and adds up the difference.
